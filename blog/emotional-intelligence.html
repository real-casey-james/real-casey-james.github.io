Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Being in touch with your emotions, the ability to distinguish between different emotions and label them appropriately, and being able to recognise the emotions of others around you.

How is it different to IQ?

IQ is a measure of ‘intelligence’ ie ‘how smart you are’. There is some debate about the effectiveness of IQ as a measure of intelligence, but the general thinking is that your IQ is set when you’re born, whereas EQ can be learnt and built upon. Research suggests EQ is a much greater indicator of happiness and success than IQ is.

Why is it important?

The ability to recognise your own emotions helps you to guide your behaviour in all situations in life – eg recognising when you need a few minutes time out. The ability to recognise other peoples emotions helps in the way you interact with them, and leads to stronger relationships and better social dynamics.
